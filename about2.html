About St. Stephen N/P Shool

FOUNDING OF SCHOOL FOR THE BROTHERS OF ST. STEPHEN

CONCEPTION

In the year 2002, in one of the general meetings of all the brothers at the novitiate at Nkwelle - Ezunaka ,a pertinent question was asked by Br. Charles Okeke on whether the congregation of the Brothers of St . Stephen is not yet ripe to open a school. This question prompted the then superior General, very Rev.Br. Joseph Chukwuakpu to immediately constitute a five man committee who would brain- storm on how to achieve this dream. Members of the committee included:

Br. Charles Okeke - chairman

Br. Christian Umegbolu - secretary

Br. Pantaleon Okafor - member

Br. Romanus Nworie - member

Br. Martin Ukanwa - member

This committee, in the course of the brain - storming exercise, toured and visited some existing schools to look at their structures and other facilities so as to give and present a tenable suggestion to the Brothers. The schools visited include the Marist comprehensive Sec. School at Nteje and Seat of wisdom secondary school at Oraifete, run by the Vincentian Fathers and brothers. In the end a comprehensive report of the findings was documented and presented to the then Superior General. Then no action was taken. The Superior very Rev. Br. Joseph Chukwuakpu left office in 2003, when Br. Pantaloon Okafor took over from him. The new Superior General left office in 2009. Like the former he did not make a move. Then in the same 2009 very Rev. Br. Charles Okeke was elected Superior General. It was the new Superior General that took the bull by the horn.

FOUNDING ST. STEPHEN’S NURSERY AND PRIMARY SCHOOLS.

Immediately Br. Charles Okeke came in as the Superior General of the congregation of the Brothers of St. Stephen in 2009 he knew that a heavy responsibility awaits him .He began to plan on possibility of opening a school for the congregation. In 2012 , in collaboration with the parishioners (because then the novitiate was an outstation of St. Williams catholic Parish, Nkwelle - Ezunaka) he began a foundation on the open field where the brothers used to hold events like burial ceremony and so on. When the parishioners became financially incapacitated, Br. Charles took it up from where they stopped, finished the foundation up to roofing. It was at the roofing stage that he went to Akwa to apply for registration and approval by the Anambra state ministry of Education.

THE PROVISIONAL INSPECTION AND APPROVAL.

By July 2013 an inspection was carried on at the site by a team of inspectors from Ogidi education zone, led by Mrs Obi the then zonal education officer. They were sent by Dr. Laz. Okafor, the then Director of Education at the ministry of Education, Awka. Before their arrival Brother Charles Okeke and his team were ready as they had assembled all the necessary materials needed for the inspection including teachers’ certificates. The inspectorate was overwhelmed by the readiness of the school, and so, a recommendation for provisional approval was made to the ministry of Education. With this recommendation St. Stephen’s Nursery and primary Schools were granted provisional approval in 2013 by the Anambra State ministry of Education.

CHOICE OF NAME FOR THE SCHOOL

Initially the school was meant to be registered with the name St. Stephen group of schools but the ministry rather changed the name to St. Stephen’s Nursery and Primary Schools and that became the official name of the schools.

AT INCEPTION

school opened with a headmistress Mrs Amaka Okafor and a female teacher Miss Miracle Ugwu, and nineteen (19) pupils in 2013 /2014 academic session. The following teachers later joined: Mrs Onyeagba, Obiji zita (Ebisike), Ejike Precious, Udeh Chinweokwu and Br. John Ukandu,BSS.

THE PAST AND PRESENT HEADTEACHERS

Mrs Amaka Okafor, as God wanted it, did not head the school more than one term. She died before the end of first term in 2013, and then Mrs Onyeagba was made the head teacher. She left in 2014, and then Mrs Charity Odo took over from her. She headed the school till 2021 and then Ike Francesca was made the head teacher. This position she holds till date.

SCHOOL MANAGERS

When the school was opened, the Superior General, very Rev. Br. Charles Okeke saw the need to appoint a representative as he could not be steady around the school, he then appointed Br. Damian Ndubuisi as acting manager. To help the headmistress in the administration of the schools. The novices were then helping both in teaching and transporting the pupils from and back to their various homes before and at the close of school each day. Later in 2015/2016 Br. Samuel Obijuru took over as manager.

BURSARS

Br. Samuel Nnanyelugo was appointed as the bursar to manage the financial affairs of the schools. He did this job religiously from 2013 to 2015 when Br. Martin Ukanwa was appointed to the post. In 2021 Br. Gabinus Afuecheta became the school bursar, and in 2022 Br. Paul Azagba was posted to take over from him.

THE CURRENT SCHOOL SIZE

To the glory of God, the school that began with just 19 pupils in 2013, today has the population of more than three hundred (300) pupils with twenty eight teaching and non-teaching staff.

PAST AND CURRENT ACHIEVEMENTS

The school had succeeded in producing 122 graduands since 2014 till date. Most of the pupils were admitted into most prestigious secondary schools in the state ranging from Federal Girls College Onitsha, Christ the King College Onitsha, Queen of the Rosary College Onitsha, All Hallow Seminary Onitsha, Francis Cardinal Arinze Secondary School Nkwelle Ezunaka among others. Our pupils have also emerged winners in quizzes and other competitions. One significant one was the AVOSCO Science Competition in 2019 in which we took 1st and 2nd positions in the regional level among 450 Schools in Onitsha Archdiocese. In recent moral education championship examination set for primary 3, our pupils emerged 1st and 2nd for Basic3. Not only the intellectual aspect of human life does the schools care for, the school also care for spiritual aspect of our pupil’s life , because we strive for a holistic training of the pupils. The pupils are taught catechism and are guided to receive Holy Communion before passing out. So far, the schools have metamorphosed into a great school known by their products and changes in their past and present pupils which has brought so much honour to the schools, and still have greater things to offer the pupils and the society at large.

The same PTD was formed for the Nursery and Primary section. At the inception of the school in 2013, Mr. Emmanuel Omeh was elected to chair the dialogue. He held this position till 2022 when Mr. Malachi Ibekwe was elected. Thus he became the second Chairman of the PTD. At this juncture it is pertinent to mention about the spiritual life and extra – curricular activities of the school. First, the School Structure.

Mission,Vision AND Motto of The School.

The Motto of the School:

    • Br. Charles Okeke who founded the school for the congregation of the Brothers of St. Stephen had to put head together with Br. Benedict Azebeokhai to coin what would be the motto of the school. In the end it was agreed that the motto would be: Education for self-reliance. The idea was to train children who would become employers of labour after empowering them with skills in the school.

 

Mission Statement

    • The mission of the school is to produce students who are capable of contributing meaningfully by their moral and disciplinary life style to better the society.

 

Vision Statement

  • The school vision is to appreciate and demonstrate the moral values needed to live as a Christian at the various stages of growth and development. The above mission and vision statements are in tandem with the motto of the school which is: Education for self-reliance, and they are in line with the National policy on Education 2004, which is to give the child opportunities for developing manipulative skills that will enable the child function effectively in the society within the limit of the child’s capacity.
  • The teachers at St. Stephen’s Nursery and Primary schools are devotedly teaching and training the children to bring out the best in them. This they do as their mission. That is, to educate, to train, and to develop. The schools arm at moulding the pupil’s character in such a way that they will be useful to themselves and to the society.

Francis Cardinal Arinze
